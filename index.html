UV Index Guide

About the UV Index

The UV Index is a measure of solar ultraviolet (UV) radiation intensity at Earth's surface, ranging from 0 to 11+. Higher numbers indicate stronger UV radiation and greater risk of harm from sun exposure. The scale helps you plan outdoor activities and choose appropriate sun protection.

UV Index Levels & Recommendations

0–2 (Low)
Minimal risk. Basic sun protection (SPF 15+) is sufficient for most.
3–5 (Moderate)
Use SPF 30 sunscreen, wear sunglasses, and consider shade during midday.
6–7 (High)
Use SPF 30+ sunscreen, wear a hat and sunglasses. Limit sun exposure around peak hours (10 AM–4 PM).
8–10 (Very High)
Use SPF 50+ sunscreen, wear UV-protective clothing, and avoid sun during midday.
11+ (Extreme)
Avoid sun exposure if possible. Take maximum protective measures.

Chart Types

UV Index Forecast
The expected strength of ultraviolet (UV) radiation based on weather forecasts, incorporating forecast cloudiness. Updated around 06:00 and 18:00 JST.
Clear Sky UV Index Forecast
The expected strength of UV radiation assuming clear-sky conditions (no clouds). Updated around 06:00 JST.
UV Index Estimate
The estimated UV Index based on the day's actual weather observation data. Updated around 18:00 JST.
